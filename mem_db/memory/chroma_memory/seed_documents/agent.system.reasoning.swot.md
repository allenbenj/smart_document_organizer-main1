SWOT Analysis Framework 
Input Data:
    Case Information:
•	Case type (e.g., felony, misdemeanor, civil)
•	Charges and allegations
•	Defendant's background and history
•	Victim's statement and evidence
•	Physical evidence and forensic analysis
    Legal Precedents:
•	Relevant statutes and case law
•	Judicial decisions and appellate court rulings
•	Legal doctrines and principles
    Prosecution/Defense Strategy:
•	ADA's or defense attorney's experience and expertise
•	Prosecution or defense theory of the case
•	Key witnesses and evidence
•	Potential weaknesses in the opponent's case
    Investigative Reports:
•	Police reports and investigative notes
•	Forensic analysis and expert opinions
•	Witness statements and interviews
SWOT Analysis Components:
    Strengths (S):
•	Evidence: The quality and quantity of physical evidence, witness statements, and forensic analysis.
•	Witnesses: The credibility and reliability of key witnesses.
•	Legal Precedents: Favorable case law and statutes.
•	Investigative Work: The thoroughness and effectiveness of the investigation.
•	Prosecution/Defense Team: The ADA or defense attorney's experience, expertise, and resources.
    Weaknesses (W):
•	Evidence: Gaps or inconsistencies in the evidence.  
•	Witnesses: Possible biases or credibility issues with key witnesses.  
•	Legal Precedents: Unfavorable case law or statutes.  
•	Investigative Work: A flawed or incomplete investigation.  
•	Prosecution/Defense Team: Limited resources, experience, or expertise.
    

Opportunities (O):
•	New Evidence: The potential for new evidence to be discovered or introduced.
•	Witness Cooperation: Cooperation from key witnesses or new witnesses coming forward.
•	Legal Developments: Changes in laws, statutes, or case law that could benefit the case.
•	Opponent's Mistakes: Mistakes or weaknesses in the opponent's case.
•	Jury Sympathy: The potential for the jury to sympathize with the defendant or victim.
    Threats (T):
•	 Opponent's Strengths: Strong evidence, witnesses, or legal precedents supporting the opponent.
•	New Evidence: The possibility of introducing new evidence could weaken the case.
•	Witness Hostility: A lack of cooperation or antagonism from key witnesses.
•	Legal Developments: Changes in laws, statutes, or case law that could weaken the case.
•	Jury Bias: The risk of the jury being biased against the defendant or victim.
AI Agent's Decision-Making Process:
•	 Data Analysis: The AI agent examines input data to identify the case's strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats.
•	• Pattern Recognition: The AI agent detects patterns in the data to uncover potential relationships among the evidence, witnesses, and legal precedents.
    Decision-Making: The AI agent uses SWOT analysis to make strategic decisions about the case, such as:
•	Which witnesses to call or subpoena.
•	Which evidence to introduce or challenge.
•	How to frame the prosecution or defense theory of the case.
•	How to address potential weaknesses in the case.
    
Recommendation Generation: The AI agent generates recommendations for the ADA or defense attorney, such as:
•	Which legal precedents to cite or challenge.
•	How to impeach or rehabilitate key witnesses.
•	Which evidence to emphasize or downplay?
•	How to address potential jury biases or sympathies.
Benefits:
•	Improved Case Evaluation: The AI agent's SWOT analysis provides a comprehensive case evaluation, identifying strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats.
•	Strategic Decision-Making: The AI agent's recommendations help the ADA or defense attorney make informed decisions about the case.
•	Enhanced Trial Preparation: The AI agent's analysis aids the ADA or defense attorney prepare for trial by identifying potential weaknesses in the opponent's case and developing effective counterstrategies.
•	Increased Efficiency: The AI agent's automation of the SWOT analysis saves time and reduces the workload for the ADA or defense attorney, allowing them to focus on higher-level strategic decisions.
